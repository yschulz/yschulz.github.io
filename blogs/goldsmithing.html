I really don't like the expression "think outside the box". Thinking outside the box makes you come up with hundreds of unfeasable solutions to your problem. However, if you define the borders of your problem's box and actually get to know what's inside, that content will solve your problem 99.99% of the time! It's about how to put the tools together, not to invent the wheel.
Remember KISS, keep it simple stupid.

Goldsmithing

Inspiration

I have to admit, I have a bad habit. Whenever I see something that entices me, I directly go in my head to the thought: "I can do that as well!"
Some may say that is an early onset of the "dad-like" antagonism of calling professionals to fix certain things, but I actually don't find the appeal in digging through waste-water to fix a broken pipe. For me, that sentiment is more rooted in the creation, trying new things and learning new skills. My inspiration to try goldsmithing for instance comes from a video I watched on YouTube. JerryRigEverything is, what his name already suggests, a creator, who tries, tests and modifies tech, but also builds and rigs almost anything. One of his projects was to build the wedding ring of his fiance out of a piece of her wheelchair. It is a really cute story and I recommend checking it out!
